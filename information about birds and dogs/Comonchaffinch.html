The chaffinch (Fringillia coelebs) commonly known as the chaffinch, also known by the names of the chaffinch and the chaffinch; includes many genera and species that are found almost all over the world, except Australia . [ 1 ] It is a small bird and resembles a sparrow . Its beak is short, strong and thick at its base. This configuration allows it to break the hard shells of the seeds that constitute its food.
